╰┈➤ Activities & Reflection

Tzu Chi Foundation

In Tzu Chi foundation, we were taught how to separate trash in six different categories: Tin, aluminium, plastic, paper, cardboard, and items with multiple components. Things with multiple components could be broken machines that need to be disassembled before disposal. Afterward, we learned that plastic can be recycled to make various items like chairs, tents, mattresses, and blankets.

It was fascinating to see how plastic bags could be reused if they didn't have seals, glue, tape, or zip locks. Bottles also need to be cleaned before being recycled properly. I found it eye-opening that we don't take enough action in Indonesia. We're often indifferent, and global warming is becoming a bigger issue. One day, we could be forced to leave our homes and become refugees.

After this experience, I’m motivated to take action. I won’t sit idly by anymore.

St Dominican Highschool

Near St Dominican Highschool, there's a service to take care of the elderly. What we did there was learn sign language, which doesn't only help deaf or mute people to communicate but it also slows the effects of dementia. Plus, we also learned a dance with them.

It turns out that volunteers and community workers are different. Community workers get paid, although it isn't too much. They get paid the amount a teacher does.
